History

The Gothic era, also known as the Middle Ages, spanned roughly from the 5th to the late 15th century. It was characterized by significant social, cultural, and architectural developments.

Society was organized under a feudal system where land was owned by nobility, and peasants worked the land in exchange for protection. The king or monarch held the highest authority, followed by lords, knights, and peasants.

Religion

The dominant influence was the Catholic Church. Religious life played a central role in medieval society, with cathedrals and monasteries serving as centers of both worship and learning. Pilgrimages were common, and the clergy held significant power.

Political Structures

Political power was often decentralized, with feudal lords holding authority over specific regions. The monarch's power varied, and the relationship between kings and nobles was complex, leading to periods of conflict.

Medieval Towns

As trade and commerce grew, medieval towns and cities began to flourish. Markets, guilds, and fairs became important economic and social hubs. The rise of a merchant class contributed to the changing social dynamics.
